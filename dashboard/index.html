Methodology

📐

Data Engineering

Merged ACN-Data (Caltech/JPL) and UrbanEV datasets. Feature engineering includes temporal encodings, rolling statistics, and demand volatility metrics.

🧠

Deep Learning Forecasting

Stacked LSTM with attention mechanism for multi-step demand forecasting. XGBoost ensemble for interpretable feature importance analysis.

🎯

Reinforcement Learning

PPO agent trained in a custom OpenAI Gym environment simulating charging station economics with demand elasticity and grid constraints.

🔄

Closed-Loop Monitoring

Automated drift detection (PSI, KS-test) with feedback-driven retraining. Dashboard alerts for anomalous revenue or utilization patterns.

🔮 Key Findings

  • UrbanEV models achieved extremely high predictive power (R² = 0.9978) due to highly regular, periodic spatial patterns across top grids.
  • XGBoost regressor outperformed Random Forest slightly, achieving the lowest overall RMSE of 0.0073 on the UrbanEV dataset.
  • For ACN-Data session-level forecasting, Random Forest achieved lower prediction errors (RMSE = 0.3283) compared to XGBoost.
  • Key features driving forecasting models include lag occupancy observations, hour-of-day, day-of-week, and queue length proxies.
  • Models are evaluated on real-world test segments using a clean temporal split to guarantee generalizability.

💰 Pricing Strategy

The Tariff Pricing Agent generates optimal dynamic pricing parameters based on predicted utilization rates:

  • Surge Pricing: Applied when predicted utilization exceeds 80%. Multiplier scales up to 1.8x.
  • Discount Pricing: Applied when predicted utilization drops below 30%. Multiplier scales down to 0.6x.
  • Peak vs Off-Peak Periods: Period segmentation (Peak, Shoulder, Off-Peak) handles different baseline characteristics.
  • Simulated Customer Response: Incorporates a price elasticity of demand (-0.30) to simulate volume shift.
  • Financial Impact: Quantifies total revenue, average utilization rates, and off-peak charging sessions compared to flat baseline.

🔄 Iterative Learning Loop

The monitoring agent tracks operational indicators across training episodes to ensure continuous learning and adaptation:

  • Episode Run Cycles: Simulates interactive dynamic pricing runs where pricing rules adjust based on previous step errors.
  • Learning Rate Tuning: Uses gradient adjustments to update the dynamic multiplier ranges and optimize total revenue.
  • Wait-Time Proxy: Tracks congestion by calculating expected wait time when utilization rises above 90%.
  • Pricing Efficiency: Defined as revenue generated per unit of energy delivered (₹/kWh).
  • Parameter Co-adaptation: Explores optimal surge and discount levels recursively, improving pricing efficiency.
